The Money Sitting in Your Estimate Folder Right Now

Most service business owners think their lead problem is about getting more leads. It is not. The bigger problem is sitting quietly in a folder of estimates that never closed.

Let me ask you something. How many estimates did you run last month? Now, how many of them closed?

If you are like most roofing, HVAC, or pool company owners, you probably closed somewhere between 25 and 35% of them. That sounds normal. It feels normal. But when you do the actual math on what the other 65 to 75% is worth, it stops feeling normal very quickly.

This is not a post about getting more leads. You do not need more leads. You need to recover the revenue that is already sitting in your pipeline, waiting.

Let's Do the Math Together

Here is a simple calculation I run for every service business I work with. Plug in your own numbers and see what comes up.

Say you run 50 estimates a month. That is not unusual for a busy roofing, HVAC, or pool company during peak season. Your average job is worth around $13,000. And your current close rate, without a structured follow-up system, sits at about 30%.

Your Current Situation
Estimates per month 50
Average job value $13,000
Close rate (no follow-up system) 30%
Jobs closed per month 15 jobs
Estimates going cold every month 35 estimates
Cold estimate value per month $455,000

Four hundred and fifty-five thousand dollars. Every month. Sitting in your estimate folder, doing nothing.

Now here is where it gets interesting. Not all of that is recoverable. Some of those homeowners found someone else. Some changed their minds. Some are not ready yet. But a significant chunk of them just never heard back from you after the estimate. And for those people, the job is still available. You just have not followed up.

What a 20% Recovery Rate Actually Looks Like

Industry data is pretty consistent on this. A structured follow-up system recovers around 20% of estimates that go cold. That is not a stretch. That is the floor, not the ceiling, when you do follow-up correctly.

Twenty percent of 35 cold estimates is 7 additional jobs per month. At $13,000 average, that is $91,000 in recovered revenue every month. Over a year, that is $1,092,000.

The leads are not the problem. The follow-up is the problem.

Why Most Companies Never Recover That Revenue

I spent three years as a lead setter inside a US roofing company. I watched this happen up close. A rep would go out, deliver a great estimate, and come back with a solid lead. Then nothing. The homeowner would go quiet. The rep would mean to follow up, but the next job came in, then another, and that estimate just sat there aging out.

It is not laziness. It is volume. When you are running 50 estimates a month, manually tracking and following up on all 35 that did not close is a full-time job on its own. Nobody has time for that on top of everything else.

So the estimates pile up. The revenue sits uncollected. And the owner assumes the lead just was not interested.

Most of the time, that is not true. The homeowner is still thinking about it. They are comparing quotes, talking to a spouse, waiting for the right moment. The company that follows up at the right time, in the right way, is the one that gets the job.

What Good Follow-Up Actually Looks Like

Good follow-up is not pestering someone. It is not five calls in three days. It is a structured, timed sequence that keeps you top of mind without making the homeowner feel pressured.

For estimates that go cold, the sequence that works looks roughly like this. A message 48 hours after the estimate that checks in without pushing. A follow-up at day 5 that adds a piece of value, answers a common question, addresses a common objection. Another touch at day 10 that makes it easy for them to re-engage. And a final message at day 14 that closes the loop cleanly without burning the relationship.

That four-touch sequence, done consistently, is what generates a 20% recovery rate. It is not complicated. But it has to happen every time, for every estimate, without fail. That is the part most companies cannot do manually at scale.

The Question Worth Asking Right Now

How much money am I losing from cold estimates? That is the question every service business owner should be able to answer. Most cannot, because they have never looked at it this way.

Go back to your numbers. How many estimates did you run last month? What is your average job value? What is your close rate? Now multiply the cold estimates by your average job value, and take 20% of that number. That is your recovery opportunity. That is the money that is already yours if you have the right system to go get it.

What is a good estimate-to-close rate for a roofing company? A healthy target is 35 to 45%. For HVAC, it is closer to 40 to 50% on replacement jobs. For pool companies with high-ticket custom builds, 25 to 35% is strong. If you are below those numbers, a follow-up system is the single fastest way to close the gap.

The revenue is there. The leads already came in, you already ran the estimate, you already did the hard part. The only thing standing between you and that money is a follow-up system that runs automatically, every time, without depending on someone remembering to make the call.
